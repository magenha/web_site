The overall purpose of this project is to develop knowledge about haplogroups found among people born primarily in Cuba, throughout Latin America, and their ancestors in Europe and Africa.
The immediate objective of the project is to develop a haplogroup database that will help users to identify people who share their haplogroup and thus develop their genealogy.
The long-term goal is to establish a relationship between the haplogroups and the lineages that gave rise to them, creating a demographic profile of the haplogroups in these societies. This will be a description of the existing haplogroups in the various regions, how frequent they are at different times and how their migrations were.

HOW CAN I HELP?

When the site construction is done, users will be able to build their genealogical tree,
upload DNA tests results and contact with each other. For now, MAGENHA urges you to continue
your family research and complement it with DNA tests.
If a person has taken a test on 23andme, can donate his/her DNA relatives list (see this video to learn download relatives list). That will help us compute some statistical results, like the ones in RESOURCES. There is no personal information in the file, nor from the relatives. Our focus is the grandparent's country of birth and haplogrup of the relatives. Once we extract the information, we delete the file. If you are willing to donate your relatives list, please contact us .

DNA tests

The most useful data will be from raw_data from 23andme or Ancestry ,
.fasta files or STR results of FTDNA.

Genealogy

The site will provide a plataform for users to develop teir genealogical tree. As the main idea is to focus on paternal and maternal lines of inheritance, the trees will only be from these lines. Algorithms will work to detect repetitions of people in the database and possible inconsistencies due to lack of documentation to provide the affiliations.

Inter-communication

An important part of research is debate and exchange. Users will be able to exchange messages, share genealogical lines and collaborate in the construction of their trees. In the same way, the consultation of bibliographic material will be encouraged.
